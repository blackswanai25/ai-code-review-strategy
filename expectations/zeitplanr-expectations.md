# Zeitplanr -- Phase 1: Expectation Mapping

Pre-exposure expectations from 5 personas representing the German market. Each persona articulates what they expect from a GDPR-compliant meeting scheduling tool BEFORE seeing the product.

---

## Jan Richter -- Teamleiter Engineering at CloudNova GmbH

*32, Berlin, remote-first startup, 8-person engineering team across Berlin/Lisbon/Bangalore. Already hitting Calendly free tier limits. Developer who evaluates tools with a critical eye.*

### Before I see this tool, I expect:

**First Screen (what should greet me):**
1. A single "Connect Google Calendar" button front and center -- I want to be synced within 10 seconds of landing, not filling out forms
2. A guided flow to create my first event type (15-min, 30-min, or 60-min), not a blank dashboard that leaves me figuring things out
3. A live preview of my booking page so I can see what my clients will see before I share anything

**Within 2 minutes of signing up, I should be able to:**
1. Connect my Google Calendar, see my existing events reflected, and have 2-way sync confirmed
2. Create at least 3 different event types (Calendly limits me to 1 on free tier -- this is exactly why I'm looking at alternatives)
3. Copy a booking link and send it to a client in Slack, with Google Meet auto-generated in the confirmation

**What would make me trust this tool immediately:**
1. Page loads in under 1 second and the UI feels modern -- if it's slow or looks like a 2018 Bootstrap template, I'm out
2. DSGVO badge or compliance notice visible on the landing page -- my clients at German corporates ask about this and I need to point them to something
3. Google Calendar sync works on the first attempt without errors, token issues, or "please re-authenticate" loops

**What would make me close the tab within 30 seconds:**
1. Google Calendar sync fails or shows a loading spinner for more than 5 seconds -- this is the entire point of the tool
2. The booking page looks unprofessional or amateurish -- I share this link with enterprise clients, it reflects on me
3. I'm forced into a free tier that only allows 1 event type -- that's exactly the Calendly limitation I'm trying to escape

**My #1 job this tool MUST do better than my current approach:**
Multiple event types without paying. Right now, Calendly locks me into 1 event type on free tier, so I use a single "30-min meeting" link for everything -- quick syncs, client demos, 1:1s. It's embarrassing to send a "30-min call" link when a client needs a 60-minute deep-dive. I need at least 3 event types (15min quick chat, 30min sync, 60min workshop) on a free or very cheap plan.

**What "good enough" looks like for me:**
Google Calendar 2-way sync, Google Meet auto-generation, 3+ event types, timezone auto-detection for guests, buffer time between meetings, and a booking page that looks clean enough to share with enterprise clients. All of this for free or under 10 EUR/month.

**What would make me recommend this to a colleague:**
If I can set up my entire scheduling workflow -- 3 event types, Google Meet links, buffer times, timezone support -- in under 5 minutes AND the booking page looks better than Calendly's. If a colleague says "nice booking page," I'm telling every founder in my network.

**What would make me cancel after the first month:**
Google Calendar sync breaks or becomes unreliable. One double-booking with a client and I'm back to Calendly with my tail between my legs. Reliability is non-negotiable.

**My biggest fear about trying this tool:**
That I'll migrate my entire booking flow, share the new links everywhere, and then discover a critical bug -- sync lag, timezone errors, missing notifications -- that makes me look unprofessional in front of clients. Switching costs are real. If I switch and it fails, I've lost credibility.

**Features I expect but won't explicitly ask for:**
1. Automatic Google Meet link generation in the calendar invite (not a separate step -- it should just happen)
2. Mobile-responsive booking page (half my clients book from their phone)
3. Email notifications that look professional and include timezone-converted times for both parties

## Petra Becker -- Projektleiterin at Maschinenbau Becker & Sohn GmbH

*44, Stuttgart, Mittelstand manufacturing, 200 employees. Lives in Outlook/Exchange. Coordinates across Konstruktion, Fertigung, Montage, and external customers. IT department must approve every tool.*

### Before I see this tool, I expect:

**First Screen (what should greet me):**
1. The website in German -- proper German, not machine-translated. If I see "Schedule your meetings" instead of "Termine einfach planen" as the headline, I already doubt this is made for the German market
2. Outlook/Exchange integration featured prominently on the landing page -- not buried under a "Google Calendar" hero section where I have to scroll to find out if my calendar is even supported
3. A clear DSGVO-Konformitat section with downloadable documentation -- my IT-Abteilung will ask for an Auftragsverarbeitungsvertrag (AVV) before they even let me test this

**Within 2 minutes of signing up, I should be able to:**
1. Connect my Outlook/Exchange calendar and see my existing Termine reflected correctly
2. Create a Jour-Fixe booking page that I can send to a customer (Herr Muller at Daimler should see a professional, branded page -- not something that looks like a Silicon Valley startup)
3. Understand the pricing clearly -- what does it cost for my team of 12, in Euros, with no surprise charges

**What would make me trust this tool immediately:**
1. An Impressum at the bottom of the page showing a German GmbH with a real address -- ideally in Deutschland, not an Irish subsidiary or a Delaware LLC
2. A list of Referenzkunden from the Mittelstand -- if Bosch Rexroth or a Maschinenbauer uses it, I feel safe. If the testimonials are all from "TechBro Inc, San Francisco," this is not for me
3. A phone number or email for German-language support -- not just a chatbot or a "community forum"

**What would make me close the tab within 30 seconds:**
1. The landing page only mentions Google Calendar and Google Meet. If Outlook/Exchange is not supported, this tool is useless for 90% of German Mittelstand companies. I'm gone immediately.
2. English-only interface with no German option. My colleagues in Fertigung do not speak English fluently enough to navigate a scheduling tool. The entire UI must be in German.
3. No Impressum, no Datenschutzerklarung link in the footer. This tells me immediately that DSGVO is an afterthought, and I cannot recommend this to our Geschaftsfuhrung.

**My #1 job this tool MUST do better than my current approach:**
Scheduling cross-department meetings with external customers. Right now, I spend 45 minutes on a single Jour-Fixe because I have to email 4 department heads, wait for responses, find overlapping slots manually, then email the customer. If I could send one link where everyone's Outlook availability is already considered, and the customer picks from pre-approved slots, I would save 3-4 hours per week minimum.

**What "good enough" looks like for me:**
Outlook/Exchange 2-way sync that my IT department approves, a German-language interface, a professional booking page I'm not embarrassed to send to Daimler or BMW procurement, and DSGVO documentation I can hand to our Datenschutzbeauftragter. If it also handles group scheduling (finding a slot for 5+ people), I would consider it transformational.

**What would make me recommend this to a colleague:**
If our IT department approves it quickly (good DSGVO documentation, AVV available), AND our customers respond positively to the booking page. When Herr Muller from Daimler says "Das ist ja praktisch, wer macht das?" -- that is the moment I tell every Projektleiter in our company.

**What would make me cancel after the first month:**
Outlook sync issues. If calendar entries appear late, duplicated, or disappear, the cascading damage in a manufacturing project timeline is enormous. One missed Jour-Fixe with a key customer because of a sync problem and I'm back to doing everything manually via email.

**My biggest fear about trying this tool:**
That I'll spend weeks convincing IT and Geschaftsfuhrung to approve this tool, train my team on it, migrate our scheduling workflow -- and then discover it's really built for Google-first startups and Outlook is a second-class citizen. Half-working Outlook integration is worse than no Outlook integration.

**Features I expect but won't explicitly ask for:**
1. Automatic meeting room booking or at least a field for "Besprechungsraum" in the invitation -- we have physical meeting rooms that need to be reserved
2. The booking page respects German date/time formatting (24-hour clock, DD.MM.YYYY) without me having to configure it
3. Calendar invitations sent in German with proper "Sie" addressing -- not "Hey Petra, your meeting is confirmed!"

## Anna Schmidt -- Assistentin der Geschaftsfuhrung at Muller Consulting AG

*29, Frankfurt, manages calendars for 3 Partners at a 500-person consulting firm. 100+ scheduling interactions per week. Power user who lives in keyboard shortcuts. Current pain: juggling 3 separate Calendly accounts.*

### Before I see this tool, I expect:

**First Screen (what should greet me):**
1. A unified dashboard where I can see all 3 Partners' calendars side by side -- not a single-user view that I have to switch between
2. Color-coded upcoming bookings (Partner A = blue, Partner B = green, Partner C = red) with today's schedule front and center
3. A pending approvals queue -- I need to review and confirm bookings before they land on a Partner's calendar, not find out after the fact

**Within 2 minutes of signing up, I should be able to:**
1. Connect all 3 Partners' Google Calendars to my single account and see their combined availability
2. Set different availability rules per Partner (Partner A: mornings only before 10:00, Partner B: never Fridays, Partner C: 30-min buffer between all meetings)
3. Create distinct booking pages per Partner with their name, photo, and appropriate meeting types

**What would make me trust this tool immediately:**
1. Multi-calendar management is a core feature, not an afterthought or an enterprise upsell buried on the pricing page
2. An approval workflow is built in -- I see "Booking request from Dr. Hoffmann for Partner A, Wednesday 09:00" and I confirm or suggest alternatives
3. The booking page looks premium and consulting-appropriate -- our clients are DAX board members; the page needs to match that gravitas

**What would make me close the tab within 30 seconds:**
1. It's clearly a single-user tool. If the signup flow asks "Connect YOUR calendar" with no option to manage others, it cannot do what I need. One calendar = useless to me.
2. No approval workflow -- if a client can auto-book a slot on Partner A's calendar without my review, that's a disaster waiting to happen. The Geschaftsfuhrung would never accept that.
3. The booking page looks generic or templated. Our clients are C-suite executives at major corporations. A booking page that looks like a free SaaS tool undermines the firm's brand.

**My #1 job this tool MUST do better than my current approach:**
Managing 3 Partners' scheduling from one place. Today I log into 3 separate Calendly accounts, cross-reference 3 Google Calendars manually, and copy-paste availability between browser tabs. When Partner B's flight is delayed and I need to reschedule 4 meetings, it takes me 45 minutes of frantic tab-switching. A single dashboard where I see all 3, drag-and-drop reschedule, and auto-notify affected parties would cut my daily scheduling time in half.

**What "good enough" looks like for me:**
One login, 3 Partners' calendars visible, per-person availability rules, booking approval before confirmation, buffer/prep time configuration, and professional booking pages. If it does these 6 things reliably, I will move all 3 Partners off Calendly this week.

**What would make me recommend this to a colleague:**
The moment I show another EA at a competing firm my single dashboard managing 3 Partners and she sees the approval queue, the color-coded calendar view, and the one-click reschedule -- she'll ask for the link before I finish the sentence.

**What would make me cancel after the first month:**
A double-booking. If Partner A and a client both show up to a call that should not have been booked, or if two clients are booked into the same slot because the sync lagged, I lose the trust of Partners who took months to onboard. One double-booking = instant cancellation and back to manual email scheduling.

**My biggest fear about trying this tool:**
That the multi-calendar feature is technically there but poorly implemented -- slow to update, confusing to navigate, permissions that break. I'm the single point of failure for 3 very busy, very demanding executives. If this tool makes me slower instead of faster, even for one day, the Partners will lose confidence in it and I'll never get a second chance to introduce a new tool.

**Features I expect but won't explicitly ask for:**
1. Keyboard shortcuts for common actions (approve booking, reschedule, switch between Partner views) -- I do this 100 times a day, every click adds up
2. A "hold" option where I can tentatively block a time slot while waiting for a Partner's verbal confirmation, then convert it to a confirmed booking
3. Smart conflict detection that warns me BEFORE a conflict happens, not after -- "Partner A has a flight landing at 14:30, the 15:00 booking may be at risk"

## David Kim -- Freelance Strategy Consultant at David Kim Consulting

*37, Koln, solo freelancer with clients across DE, UK, US, and Singapore. 4 timezones daily. Currently self-hosting Cal.com but tired of maintenance. Every euro matters. Booking page is embedded on his personal website.*

### Before I see this tool, I expect:

**First Screen (what should greet me):**
1. A clear timezone indicator -- I want to see "Your timezone: CET (UTC+1)" front and center, confirming the tool knows where I am before I do anything else
2. A rapid setup flow: connect Google Calendar, create one event type, get a shareable link AND an embed code -- four steps, done
3. A preview of my booking page as clients will see it, with timezone auto-detection shown (e.g., "Showing times in SGT for a visitor from Singapore")

**Within 2 minutes of signing up, I should be able to:**
1. Connect my Google Calendar and create a "60-min Strategy Call" event type with Google Meet as default but Zoom as an option the client can choose
2. Set my available hours (08:00-10:00 CET for Asia clients, 15:00-18:00 CET for European clients, 20:00-22:00 CET for US East Coast) with a daily limit of 4 calls
3. Copy an embed code (iframe or JS snippet) that I can paste into my website, and copy a direct booking link for LinkedIn messages

**What would make me trust this tool immediately:**
1. Timezone handling works perfectly on the first test -- I open my own booking link in an incognito window, it detects my timezone, and all slots show correctly converted times
2. DSGVO compliant with an Impressum on the site -- as a German Freiberufler, I'm legally required to ensure my tools comply, and I need to point my Datenschutzerklarung to theirs
3. Transparent pricing with a genuinely usable free tier or a solo plan under 10 EUR/month -- I've evaluated Calendly, SavvyCal, TidyCal, and Cal.com. I know exactly what the market charges.

**What would make me close the tab within 30 seconds:**
1. Timezone handling is wrong or confusing. If I set availability at 09:00 CET and a client in Singapore sees 09:00 SGT, this tool is dangerous and I will never trust it. Timezone accuracy is existential for my business.
2. No embeddable widget. My booking flow lives on davidkim.consulting -- if I can only share a link to their hosted page, it breaks my client journey and I look less professional.
3. Google Meet only, no Zoom support. Half my clients (US, Asia) use Zoom. Forcing them onto Google Meet adds friction to an already complex cross-timezone relationship.

**My #1 job this tool MUST do better than my current approach:**
Timezone handling without any mental overhead. Right now with Cal.com self-hosted, I still get timezone edge cases around DST transitions -- a client in the US booked a slot that was correct last week but wrong this week because US and EU DST switch on different dates. I need a tool that handles IANA timezone databases, DST transitions for every region, and shows the guest their LOCAL time with zero ambiguity. I do the timezone math in my head 20 times a day. I want to stop.

**What "good enough" looks like for me:**
Bulletproof timezone handling, embeddable widget that matches my website's look, Google Meet + Zoom as client-selectable options, daily meeting limits (max 4 calls), focus time blocks (10:00-14:00 sacred), and DSGVO compliance. Free or under 8 EUR/month for a solo user.

**What would make me recommend this to a colleague:**
If a client in Singapore books a call at what they see as 16:00 SGT, I see it as 09:00 CET, Google Meet fires up, and neither of us had to think about timezone math -- I'll write a LinkedIn post about it that afternoon. Perfect timezone UX is so rare that when it works, it feels like magic.

**What would make me cancel after the first month:**
A single timezone error that causes a missed or awkward client call. If my Singapore client shows up at the wrong time because of a DST conversion bug, I've wasted their time, looked unprofessional, and potentially lost a 5-figure engagement. I'll go back to self-hosting Cal.com within the hour.

**My biggest fear about trying this tool:**
Vendor lock-in combined with reliability risk. I'm replacing a self-hosted solution I control with a SaaS I don't. If Zeitplanr goes down during a booking-heavy week, or if they change pricing after I've migrated my website embed, I'm stuck. I need to know there's an export path and that the embed won't break if I decide to leave.

**Features I expect but won't explicitly ask for:**
1. Automatic DST adjustment that accounts for different DST transition dates across regions (US, EU, Australia all switch on different weekends)
2. A booking confirmation email that shows the time in BOTH my timezone and the client's timezone, so neither of us has to do math
3. Client self-service rescheduling with a single click from the confirmation email -- I don't want to be a human scheduling middleware

That I'll onboard 8 interviewers -- each of whom is a senior engineer or manager with limited patience for new tools -- and the experience will be clunky, buggy, or confusing. If even one interviewer says "this doesn't work, I'm not using it," the whole system falls apart because round-robin needs everyone participating. Getting 8 busy people to adopt a new tool is a one-shot opportunity.

### Immediate Risk Flags for Zeitplanr

1. **Petra will bounce instantly** if the product is Google-only. The product description mentions "Google Calendar sync, Google Meet integration" with no mention of Outlook/Exchange. If this is accurate, the entire Mittelstand segment is lost.
