# Auftragr -- Phase 1: Expectation Mapping

Pre-exposure expectations from 5 user personas, articulated before seeing the product.

---

## Klaus Weber -- Geschaeftsfuehrer at Weber Elektrotechnik GmbH

*55, Erlangen. 15-person Handwerksbetrieb, Elektrotechnik. EUR 2.5M revenue. On job sites until 14:00, does everything himself.*

### Before I see this tool, I expect:

**First Screen (what should greet me):**
1. A plain, short list of Elektrotechnik tenders near Erlangen -- no dashboard, no graphs, no complexity. Just: "Hier sind Ausschreibungen fuer Sie."
2. Each tender as one line: project name, Ort, ungefaehre Auftragssumme, Abgabefrist. That is it. I do not want to think.
3. It must load fast and look right on my Samsung phone. I will be looking at this on the Baustelle between two installations with plaster dust on my fingers.

**Within 2 minutes of signing up, I should be able to:**
1. Enter "Elektrotechnik" and "Erlangen, 50km" and immediately see matching tenders -- no 20-field company profile form.
2. Tap on one tender and read a one-screen summary: what is the job, where, how big, when is Submission.
3. Set up an email or WhatsApp alert so I stop having to remember to check portals.

**What would make me trust this tool immediately:**
1. It is entirely in German. Not "powered by AI" in English on every button. German menus, German explanations, German support.
2. There is a phone number I can call if something does not work. A real person, ideally someone who understands what a Handwerksbetrieb needs.
3. The Handwerkskammer or Kreishandwerkerschaft has mentioned it, or another Elektro-Meister I know uses it.

**What would make me close the tab within 30 seconds:**
1. A registration form asking for Umsatzsteuer-ID, DUNS number, company size, and 15 other fields before I can see a single Ausschreibung.
2. An English-language interface or a dashboard full of charts, KPIs, and "pipeline stages" -- I run a Handwerksbetrieb, not a DAX-Konzern.
3. A pricing page showing EUR 200/month before I even know if the tool finds tenders I care about.

**My #1 job this tool MUST do better than my current approach:**
Stop me from missing relevant Ausschreibungen because I forgot to check Vergabe.bayern.de for three days. Right now I check when I remember, usually at 21:00 on my phone, and half the time the interesting ones already have a Submissionsfrist in 5 days that I cannot meet. This tool needs to find them for me and send me a Nachricht the day they are published.

**What "good enough" looks like for me:**
If it reliably sends me an alert for every Elektro-Ausschreibung within 50km of Erlangen, and I catch even one additional tender per quarter that I would have missed, it is worth EUR 30/month. One won tender at my margins easily pays for the entire year.

**What would make me recommend this to a colleague:**
When I tell my Meisterkollege at the Innungsversammlung: "I got a WhatsApp from this tool on Monday about an Ausschreibung for the new Schulzentrum in Forchheim, bid on it, and won. Would have never seen it otherwise." That is a story I would tell.

**What would make me cancel after the first month:**
If the alerts send me Hochbau tenders, HLK tenders, or Ausschreibungen in Passau. Irrelevant noise would make me ignore the alerts within a week, and then the tool is worthless.

**My biggest fear about trying this tool:**
That I spend time setting it up, learn a new system, and then it is just another portal I have to check. I already have too many logins. If this adds complexity instead of removing it, I am out.

**Features I expect but won't explicitly ask for:**
1. Automatic deadline reminders -- 7 days and 2 days before Submissionsfrist -- because I will forget.
2. A way to see the tender on my phone without downloading a 50-page PDF over mobile data.
3. That my data is stored in Germany and that nobody sells my company information to competitors or spams me.

## Thomas Berger -- Einkaufsleiter at Berger & Soehne Bau GmbH

*48, Nuernberg. 150-person Bauunternehmen, Hochbau. EUR 30M revenue. Scans 3 portals every morning, manages Excel tracker.*

### Before I see this tool, I expect:

**First Screen (what should greet me):**
1. A dashboard showing "12 neue Ausschreibungen seit gestern" with my saved Suchprofil already applied -- Rohbau/Stahlbeton/Mauerwerk, Raum Nuernberg 150km, Auftragswert ab EUR 200K.
2. Clear filters at the top: Gewerk (CPV-Code), Region, Auftragswert, Submissionsfrist -- the same dimensions I use every morning to scan.
3. A counter distinguishing new versus already-reviewed tenders. I scan 80 per week; I need to know what I have already seen.

**Within 2 minutes of signing up, I should be able to:**
1. Create a Suchprofil with my Gewerke (CPV codes for Rohbau, Stahlbeton, Mauerwerk), my region (150km um Nuernberg), and my minimum Auftragswert (EUR 200K).
2. See today's matching results from DTVP, Vergabe24, and TED in one unified list -- not three separate tabs.
3. Click on a tender and see the key facts extracted: Auftraggeber, Auftragswert, Submissionsfrist, Lose, and whether the Leistungsverzeichnis is downloadable.

**What would make me trust this tool immediately:**
1. Data freshness indicator: "Stand: heute, 06:30 Uhr" on every listing. If I see yesterday's date, I close the tab. My current process catches things at 07:00; this must be faster.
2. Source transparency: each tender shows which portal it came from (DTVP, TED, Vergabe24) with a direct link to the original listing. I need to verify.
3. Correct CPV-Code mapping. If I search for 45262000 (Stahlbetonarbeiten) and get results for 45310000 (Elektroinstallation), the tool is useless.

**What would make me close the tab within 30 seconds:**
1. No Gewerk or CPV-Code filter. If I cannot filter by my specific trades, this is just another portal aggregator that makes me scroll through hundreds of irrelevant listings.
2. Stale data -- if I see tenders with Submissionsfristen that already passed, or listings from last week showing as "new."
3. No way to mark tenders as "angesehen," "weitergeleitet an Kalkulation," or "abgelehnt." Without status tracking, I am back to my Excel.

**My #1 job this tool MUST do better than my current approach:**
Replace my 90-minute morning ritual of logging into three portals, downloading PDFs, scanning each Leistungsverzeichnis, and copy-pasting key data into my Excel tracker. Right now I check DTVP, then Vergabe24, then TED, each with its own login and terrible search. If this tool gives me one unified, pre-filtered, up-to-date list every morning, it saves me 7.5 hours per week.

**What "good enough" looks like for me:**
If it covers at least DTVP and Vergabe24 with correct CPV filtering, updates by 07:00 daily, and lets me forward tenders to my Kalkulatorin with one click, it is worth EUR 150/month easily. That is two hours of my salary per month for 7+ hours saved per week.

**What would make me recommend this to a colleague:**
If I open it Monday morning and see a complete, clean list of everything relevant published since Friday -- across all portals -- with Submissionsfristen highlighted in red/yellow/green. Then I forward three to Kalkulation in under 10 minutes. That replaces my entire Monday morning.

**What would make me cancel after the first month:**
If I discover it is missing tenders that I find on DTVP manually. Incomplete coverage is worse than no tool, because I would still need to check portals AND check this tool. It must be a complete replacement, not an addition.

**My biggest fear about trying this tool:**
That it misses a tender and I only find out when a competitor wins a job I should have seen. In public procurement, if you miss the Submissionsfrist by one day, you are out. There is no second chance. The tool must have complete coverage or I cannot trust it.

**Features I expect but won't explicitly ask for:**
1. Automatic de-duplication of tenders that appear on multiple portals (the same Ausschreibung on DTVP and TED should appear once, not twice).
2. A way to share a tender with my Kalkulatorin with internal notes attached -- "Sabine, bitte anschauen, sieht nach unserem Profil aus."
3. That the Suchprofil is editable without starting over -- as our company takes on new Gewerke or enters new regions, I need to adjust.

## Lisa Hartmann -- BIM-Managerin / Leiterin Digitalisierung at MegaBau AG

*35, Muenchen. 500+ employee Generalunternehmer, Infrastruktur. EUR 120M revenue. Evaluates digital tools professionally.*

### Before I see this tool, I expect:

**First Screen (what should greet me):**
1. A pipeline overview: how many tenders are in each stage (New, Evaluating, Bidding, Submitted, Awarded, Lost) -- a funnel I can show to the Geschaeftsfuehrung in our Monday meeting.
2. Key metrics: current pipeline value, win rate trend, average response time, number of active bids. Not vanity metrics -- real operational KPIs.
3. Team activity: who reviewed what, which tenders are unassigned, where are bottlenecks. We have 6 people in BD; I need to see their workload.

**Within 2 minutes of signing up, I should be able to:**
1. Understand the integration capabilities: is there an API? Can I connect this to our Nevaris ERP and Power BI? Where is the developer documentation?
2. Create a test Suchprofil and evaluate the matching quality against our known active tenders -- I want to see if it finds projects we already know about.
3. Check the security and compliance page: DSGVO documentation, data processing agreement (AVV), hosting location, ISO certifications.

**What would make me trust this tool immediately:**
1. Published API documentation with clear endpoints, authentication methods, and rate limits. If they have an API, they are serious about enterprise use.
2. DSGVO-compliant data processing agreement (Auftragsverarbeitungsvertrag) available for download without having to ask sales.
3. Role-based access control visible in the settings -- Admin, Einkauf, Kalkulation, Geschaeftsfuehrung roles. Not just "user" and "admin."

**What would make me close the tab within 30 seconds:**
1. No multi-user support. If this is a single-user tool, it is a toy. We need 15+ seats with different permission levels.
2. No data export of any kind. If our data goes in but cannot come out, this is vendor lock-in and I will not recommend it.
3. A landing page full of marketing buzzwords ("AI-powered," "revolutionary," "disruptive") but no product screenshots, no documentation, no pricing transparency.

**My #1 job this tool MUST do better than my current approach:**
Replace our dying Access database that the BD team uses to track the tender pipeline. Right now, 6 people enter data into a shared Access file on a network drive. It crashes weekly, has no real-time collaboration, no analytics, and no connection to our other systems. I need a modern, multi-user, API-connected tender management platform that I can integrate into our digital ecosystem (ERP, BI, BIM coordination).

**What "good enough" looks like for me:**
If it provides reliable tender discovery, multi-user pipeline tracking with role-based access, and a REST API that I can connect to Power BI for reporting -- that replaces the Access database and justifies EUR 500-800/month for our team. ROI is easy to prove if it reduces missed tenders and improves our win rate by even 2%.

**What would make me recommend this to a colleague:**
When I present to the Geschaeftsfuehrung a Power BI dashboard that pulls live pipeline data from Auftragr's API, showing our tender funnel, win rates by region and Gewerk, and projected pipeline value -- and they realize this was built in two weeks instead of the six-month custom development they expected.

**What would make me cancel after the first month:**
If the API is "coming soon," if data export is limited to PDF only, or if the product roadmap shows no interest in enterprise features. I am evaluating this for a 500-person company. If the vendor is building for freelancers and Handwerksbetriebe only, the tool will never grow with us.

**My biggest fear about trying this tool:**
That I champion this internally, convince the Geschaeftsfuehrung to invest, migrate the BD team onto it, and then the startup behind it runs out of funding or pivots. I need to see financial stability signals -- funding, customer count, data escrow provisions. I have been burned before by PropTech startups that disappeared.

**Features I expect but won't explicitly ask for:**
1. SSO integration (Azure AD / SAML) -- our IT policy requires it for any SaaS tool with company data.
2. Audit log of who changed what and when -- for compliance and internal accountability.
3. Webhook support for real-time event notifications to our internal systems, not just email alerts.

## Sabine Mueller -- Kalkulatorin at Tiefbau Franken GmbH

*42, Wuerzburg. 80-person Tiefbau firm. EUR 18M revenue. Receives tenders from Einkauf, lives in GAEB files and iTWO.*

### Before I see this tool, I expect:

**First Screen (what should greet me):**
1. Only tenders that have already been pre-filtered for Tiefbau and assigned to me by Einkauf. I do not want to discover tenders -- I want to see "Sabine, hier sind 4 neue Ausschreibungen zur Kalkulation."
2. For each tender: a compact scope summary (Erdarbeiten, Kanalbau, Strassenbau -- not just a project title), whether a GAEB-LV is available, number of Positionen, and days until Submissionsfrist.
3. A clear indicator of LV format: GAEB P83, P84, PDF-only, or not yet available. This determines whether I can even start the Kalkulation efficiently.

**Within 2 minutes of signing up, I should be able to:**
1. Open a tender and preview the Leistungsverzeichnis structure -- Titel, Lose, OZ-Gruppen -- without downloading and importing into iTWO first.
2. See whether the LV has clearly defined Positionen or vague Pauschalpreise that signal risk and extra estimation effort.
3. Mark a tender as "Kalkulation gestartet" or "Nicht unser Gewerk" so Einkauf knows I have reviewed it.

**What would make me trust this tool immediately:**
1. Correct GAEB file handling. If I can preview a P83 file and the Positionen, Mengen, and Einheiten display correctly, then the developers understand my world.
2. Technical depth in the tender summary -- not just "Tiefbauarbeiten in Wuerzburg" but "Kanalbau DN300-DN600, ca. 1.200m, offene Bauweise, Verbau erforderlich." That level of detail tells me the AI actually read the LV.
3. The ability to see similar past tenders: "This is similar to Kanalarbeiten Veitshöchheim from March 2025 where your Kalkulation was EUR 1.2M." That would be genuinely useful.

**What would make me close the tab within 30 seconds:**
1. No GAEB support at all. If I cannot see or download the P83 file from within the tool, it adds a step instead of removing one.
2. Oversimplified tender descriptions that hide the technical scope. "Bauarbeiten" tells me nothing. I need to know the Gewerk, the key Positionen, and the Mengen.
3. A tool clearly designed for Einkauf/Vertrieb people with no understanding of what a Kalkulatorin needs. Pipeline stages and win rates are not my job -- LV analysis is.

**My #1 job this tool MUST do better than my current approach:**
Stop me from wasting time on tenders that are not our scope. Right now, Einkauf forwards me 3-5 Ausschreibungen per day, and at least one of them is Hochbau or something we do not do. I open the LV, spend 20 minutes reading it, and then tell them "Das ist nicht unser Gewerk." If this tool pre-screens with accurate Gewerk classification and gives me a real scope summary, I save 1-2 hours per week of wasted Kalkulation time.

**What "good enough" looks like for me:**
If it shows me a GAEB LV preview with OZ structure, gives me an accurate scope summary so I know in 30 seconds whether this is our type of project, and lets me communicate back to Einkauf with a status -- that is worth my time. I would not pay for it directly (Einkauf buys the tools), but I would tell Thomas it is useful, which is what matters.

**What would make me recommend this to a colleague:**
If the AI summary correctly identifies that a Tiefbau-Ausschreibung includes unusual Positionen -- for example, Spezialtiefbau or Altlastensanierung that we are not equipped for -- and flags it before I waste time on the full Kalkulation. That kind of intelligent pre-screening would be a genuine "das musst du dir anschauen" moment.

**What would make me cancel after the first month:**
If the GAEB preview is buggy, displays wrong Mengen or Einheiten, or cannot handle P84 files. In my world, a wrong number is not a minor bug -- it can lead to a Kalkulationsfehler in a real Angebot. If I cannot trust the data display, I cannot use the tool.

**My biggest fear about trying this tool:**
That it gives me a false sense of security. If the AI says "Tiefbau, passt" and I skip my own review, but it missed that 30% of the LV is Landschaftsbau that we subcontract -- that mistake costs real money. I need to trust the AI classification, and trust takes time to build.

**Features I expect but won't explicitly ask for:**
1. That the LV preview handles GAEB encoding correctly -- Umlaute, Sonderzeichen, long Positionstexte. Broken character encoding is a red flag that the developers have never worked with real GAEB files.
2. A way to annotate specific Positionen within the tool: "Position 02.03.0040: Preis pruefen, letzte Kalkulation war EUR 85/m3."
3. That the tool respects Vergaberecht confidentiality -- my Kalkulation data and internal notes must never be visible to other companies using the same platform.

## Michael Schneider -- Vertriebsleiter at Fassadenbau Schneider & Partner GmbH

*38, Stuttgart. 40-person Fassadenbau specialty subcontractor. EUR 8M revenue. 50% public tenders, 50% GU-Anfragen.*

### Before I see this tool, I expect:

**First Screen (what should greet me):**
1. A unified pipeline view showing both public Ausschreibungen and my GU-Anfragen in one list. My business is half public, half private -- a tool that only shows public tenders shows me half the picture.
2. Pipeline stages I can customize: Entdeckt, In Pruefung, Angebot in Arbeit, Abgegeben, Zuschlag, Verloren. With the total pipeline value for each stage.
3. Quick filters: by source (oeffentlich vs. GU-Anfrage), by region, by Fassadentyp (Elementfassade, WDVS, Pfosten-Riegel), by project value.

**Within 2 minutes of signing up, I should be able to:**
1. Set up a Suchprofil for Fassadenbau/WDVS tenders in Sueddeutschland and see current matches from DTVP and TED.
2. Manually add a GU-Anfrage I received this morning from Zublin so I can track it alongside the public tenders.
3. Move a tender through pipeline stages with one click and add a note: "Ansprechpartner bei Zublin: Hr. Fischer, 0711-xxx."

**What would make me trust this tool immediately:**
1. A professional, clean UI that I would not be embarrassed to show on screen during a Vergabeverhandlung or GU meeting. My current Excel is embarrassing enough.
2. Salesforce integration or at least a robust CSV export that maps to my CRM fields. I cannot maintain two separate systems.
3. Reliable, timely notifications. If a new Fassade-Ausschreibung is published on DTVP and I get an alert within 2 hours, that is a signal that the system works.

**What would make me close the tab within 30 seconds:**
1. Public tenders only, with no way to add private opportunities. That immediately tells me the product was not built for subcontractors who live in both worlds.
2. An amateurish UI -- bad typography, inconsistent spacing, default Bootstrap look. I show my screen to GU partners; the tool reflects on my company.
3. No mobile access. I am at GU offices, at Messen, at Baustellen -- I need to check my pipeline and respond to GU-Anfragen from my phone.

**My #1 job this tool MUST do better than my current approach:**
Give me one unified pipeline across public tenders and GU-Anfragen. Right now I have an Excel tracker for public tenders and Salesforce for GU relationships, and they do not talk to each other. I cannot answer the basic question "What is our total pipeline value right now?" without 20 minutes of manual reconciliation. If this tool is the single source of truth for all opportunities, it changes how I run my team.

**What "good enough" looks like for me:**
If it reliably discovers Fassadenbau-Ausschreibungen from DTVP and TED, lets me add GU-Anfragen manually, tracks pipeline stages, and exports cleanly to Salesforce CSV -- that is worth EUR 100-150/month. If it actually integrates with Salesforce bidirectionally, EUR 200+.

**What would make me recommend this to a colleague:**
If after 3 months I can pull up a report showing: "Q1 2026: 45 opportunities tracked, EUR 12M pipeline, 28% win rate, best GU partner: Zublin (4 awards)." That level of business intelligence from one tool would make every Vertriebsleiter in my Netzwerk jealous.

**What would make me cancel after the first month:**
If adding GU-Anfragen manually is clunky -- too many required fields, no quick-add, slow interface. If the manual entry side is painful, I will just keep using Salesforce for private opportunities and then this tool only covers half my business, which defeats the purpose.

**My biggest fear about trying this tool:**
That it is built primarily for large Generalunternehmer or Handwerksbetriebe and does not understand the specialty subcontractor workflow. Fassadenbau is niche -- we respond to GU-Anfragen with tight deadlines, we need to track which GU sent us which inquiry, and we need to manage relationships alongside tender discovery. A tool that treats us as just another "Bauunternehmen" will not fit.

**Features I expect but won't explicitly ask for:**
1. Duplicate detection: if the same project appears as a public Ausschreibung AND I receive a GU-Anfrage for it, the tool should flag the overlap.
2. A contact/relationship layer: which GUs have I worked with, what was our history, who is the Ansprechpartner -- linked to the tenders, not separate.
3. Some form of analytics on my response times: "Average time from tender discovery to Angebotsabgabe: 8 days" -- because speed is a competitive advantage in Fassadenbau.
